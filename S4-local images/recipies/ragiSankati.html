Ragi Sankati

Description

Ragi Sankati is a nutritious and wholesome dish made from ragi (finger millet) flour, often enjoyed as a main course in Guntur. This dish is a testament to the region’s dedication to health-conscious foods, as ragi is rich in calcium, fiber, and iron. Ragi Sankati is typically served with chutneys or curries, offering a filling and satisfying meal.

To make Ragi Sankati, rice is first cooked with extra water, then ragi flour is added and mixed until a smooth, thick consistency is achieved. The mixture is then shaped into balls and served with spicy chutneys or curries. This dish showcases the versatility of ragi and its importance in Andhra cuisine, making it a staple for those who prioritize nutritious, hearty meals.

Recipe

Ingredients

• 1 cup ragi flour • 1/2 cup rice • Salt to taste

Instructions

• Cook rice with extra water, add ragi flour, and stir until thick. • Shape into balls and serve with chutney.
