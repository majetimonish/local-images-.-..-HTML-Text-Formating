Gutti Vankaya Kura

Description

Gutti Vankaya Kura is a signature dish in Guntur’s culinary scene, revered for its complex flavors and intricate preparation. “Gutti” means “stuffed” in Telugu, and this curry features small brinjals (eggplants) stuffed with a flavorful blend of spices, nuts, and aromatics. The dish is a testament to the culinary artistry of Guntur, as each brinjal is carefully slit and filled, ensuring that every bite bursts with flavor.

The stuffing mixture includes roasted sesame seeds, peanuts, red chili powder, and coriander seeds, all ground into a smooth paste. This paste is stuffed into each brinjal, which is then sautéed and simmered in a spiced tomato-onion base until tender. The brinjals absorb the sauce, becoming soft and flavorful. Gutti Vankaya Kura pairs exceptionally well with steamed rice or rotis, and it’s a true favorite during festive occasions. This dish encapsulates the rich, spice-forward essence of Guntur’s cuisine, making it a must-try for those who love bold, aromatic curries.

Recipe

Ingredients

• 10 small brinjals • 2 tbsp oil • 1 tsp mustard seeds • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder • Salt to taste • 1 tbsp roasted peanuts • 1 tbsp sesame seeds • 1 tsp red chili powder • 1 tsp coriander powder

Instructions

• Grind stuffing ingredients into a fine powder and mix with salt. • Slit each brinjal, stuff with the spice mix, and set aside. • In a pan, heat oil, add mustard seeds, and let them splutter. • Add stuffed brinjals, cover, and cook until tender, stirring occasionally.
