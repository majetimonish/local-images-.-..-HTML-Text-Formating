Ulava Charu

Description

Ulava Charu is a hearty, traditional stew beloved in Guntur and across Andhra Pradesh, known for its earthy flavors and deep, rich texture. Made from horse gram (ulavalu), a highly nutritious legume, Ulava Charu is both comforting and nourishing, perfect for cooler days or festive meals. Horse gram is a protein-rich ingredient, and its distinctive taste gives Ulava Charu a unique profile that stands out in the diverse Andhra cuisine.

To prepare Ulava Charu, horse gram is soaked overnight and then pressure-cooked to extract its flavor. The cooked gram is mashed and simmered with water, allowing it to reach a thick, velvety consistency. The stew is then seasoned with spices like garlic, red chili powder, and curry leaves, creating a warm, spicy kick that pairs beautifully with the natural nuttiness of the gram. Some families add a dash of tamarind for tang, balancing the flavors perfectly. Traditionally served with rice and a dollop of ghee, Ulava Charu is a dish that fills the stomach and the soul.

Recipe

Ingredients

• 1 cup horse gram • 4 cups water • 1 medium onion, finely chopped • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste • 1 tsp red chili powder • Salt to taste • 1 tbsp ghee • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions

• Soak the horse gram overnight, then cook in a pressure cooker until soft. • In a pan, heat ghee, add onions, and sauté until golden. • Add ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, and salt. • Mix the cooked horse gram and simmer until thickened. • Garnish with coriander and serve with rice.
